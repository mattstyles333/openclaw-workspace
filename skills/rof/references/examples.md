## Example 1: Product Purchase Decision (Phone)

**Trigger:** `ROF: best phone to buy?`

### Report Output:

```markdown
# 🎯 Best Phone to Buy - ROF Report

## TL;DR
Get the Pixel 10 Pro now (~£900) for best camera + clean Android, or wait 3 weeks for Nothing Phone 4a (~£400) if value and uniqueness matter more.

## Executive Summary
| Option | Best For | Price | Verdict |
|--------|----------|-------|---------|
| Pixel 10 Pro | Camera king, pure Android | ~£900 | Buy now ✅ |
| Nothing Phone (4a) | Value, uniqueness | ~£400 | Wait 3 weeks ⏱️ |
| Galaxy S25 Ultra | Business power user | ~£1,200 | Skip ❌ |

## Key Metrics That Matter (For Your Use Case)
| Criteria | Priority | Pixel 10 Pro | Nothing (4a) | S25 Ultra |
|----------|----------|--------------|--------------|-----------|
| Camera Quality | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | ✅ Best | 🟡 Good | ✅ Excellent |
| eSIM Support | Must have | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes |
| Wireless Charging | Nice | ✅ Qi2 | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes |
| Size/Weight | Prefer small | 🟡 Big | ✅ Compact | ❌ Huge |
| Value | Important | 🟡 £900 | ✅ £400 | ❌ £1,200 |

## Deep Dive

### Google Pixel 10 Pro (Confidence: HIGH)
**Why it wins:**
- Computational photography is point-and-shoot excellent
- Clean Android 16, 7 years updates (through 2032)
- Qi2 magnetic charging

**The catch:** £900. But "value is important" ≠ "cheap." This holds value.

### Nothing Phone (4a) - WAIT FOR THIS (Confidence: MEDIUM)
**Launch:** March 5 event, March 12 release

**Why it might be perfect:**
- Price: ~£400 (confirmed leaks)
- Triple 50MP cameras, 70x zoom
- Glyph Interface LEDs for notifications

**The risk:** New unproven camera system. Early adopter risk.

### Skip: Galaxy S25 Ultra (Confidence: HIGH)
**Why not:**
- Overkill for "light usage"
- Massive size (you want smaller)
- £1,200+ for features you won't use

## ⚠️ Devil's Advocate: What Could Go Wrong

1. **"Pixel camera is overhyped"** - Competitors caught up. But for point-and-shoot? Still wins.
2. **"Nothing will be buggy"** - New camera system, small company. Wait for reviews.
3. **"You're overthinking"** - £400 phone = £1,200 phone for 95% of use cases.

## 💡 Recommendations

**Option A: Buy Pixel 10 Pro Now**
- **Do this if:** You need it this week, camera is critical
- **Cost:** ~£900
- **Action:** Order today

**Option B: Wait for Nothing Phone 4a**
- **Do this if:** You want maximum value, like unique gadgets
- **Cost:** ~£400 (saves £500)
- **Action:** Set calendar reminder for March 12

**My Pick for You:** Wait for Nothing Phone 4a
- £500 savings is significant
- Camera will be "good enough" for light use
- 3 weeks is tolerable

**Backup plan:** If Nothing reviews poorly, buy Pixel 10 Pro on March 13.

## 📚 Sources & Confidence
| Claim | Confidence | Source |
|-------|------------|--------|
| Pixel 10 Pro best camera | ✅ High | Tech Advisor, CNET, PCMag consensus |
| Nothing Phone March 12 | ✅ High | 9to5Google, Notebookcheck leaks |
| Nothing price ~£400 | ✅ High | Dealabs leak |
| Nothing camera quality | ⚠️ Medium | Unproven until reviews |
```

## Example 2: Business Strategy (Spex4Less Improvements)

**Trigger:** `ROF: spex4less improvement and profit optimization`

### Report Output:

```markdown
# 🎯 Spex4Less Profit Optimization - ROF Report

## TL;DR
Deploy quick wins (abandoned cart + referral program) for immediate 10-15% revenue lift. Then pilot subscription model for recurring revenue and 2x customer LTV.

## Executive Summary
| Initiative | Impact | Effort | Timeline | Priority |
|------------|--------|--------|----------|----------|
| Abandoned cart recovery | High | Low | 1 week | 🔥 P0 |
| Referral program | High | Medium | 2 weeks | 🔥 P0 |
| Subscription model | Very High | Medium | 1 month | 🎯 P1 |
| B2B corporate | Medium | High | 2 months | 📅 P2 |

## Current Position (Confidence: HIGH)
**Assets to Protect:**
- Trustpilot 4.8★ (29k reviews) - social proof gold
- In-house UK lab - speed + "Made in UK" story
- 500k+ customer base - reactivation goldmine
- Family-owned positioning - trust edge over Specsavers/Boots

## Deep Dive: Top Opportunities

### 1. Abandoned Cart Recovery (Confidence: HIGH)
**Current:** Industry avg 70% abandonment in eyewear

**Quick wins:**
- Exit-intent popup with 10% off
- 3-email sequence: 1hr, 24hr, 72hr
- SMS for £150+ carts

**Impact:** 15-25% recovery rate = immediate revenue

### 2. Referral Program (Confidence: HIGH)
**Current:** Not visible on site
**Opportunity:** Happy customers (4.8★) are your army

**Launch:**
- "Give £15, Get £15" dual-sided
- Post-purchase trigger (7 days after delivery)
- Ambassador tier for 5+ referrals (25% off lifetime)

### 3. Subscription Model (Confidence: MEDIUM)
**What:** Contact lens subscriptions, lens supplies, annual frame refresh

**Spex4Less Club:** £5/month for 20% off + free cleaning kits + priority dispatch

**Impact:** Recurring revenue smooths cash flow; LTV doubles

## ⚠️ Devil's Advocate: What Could Go Wrong

1. **"Subscription fatigue"** - Customers already have too many subscriptions
2. **"Referral programs attract deal-seekers"** - Low-quality customers, high churn
3. **"Abandoned cart emails annoy customers"** - Brand damage risk
4. **"Specsavers copies you"** - They have 42% market share, can outspend

**Mitigations:**
- Subscription: Start with high-value segment only
- Referral: Quality controls (min purchase thresholds)
- Abandoned cart: Respect opt-outs, limit frequency

## 💡 Strategic Options

**Option A: Quick Wins First (Recommended)**
- Deploy abandoned cart + referral (4 weeks)
- Measure impact
- Then invest in subscription
- **Risk:** Low | **Return:** 10-15% revenue lift

**Option B: Go Big on Subscription**
- Launch Spex4Less Club immediately
- Heavy marketing investment
- **Risk:** Medium | **Return:** 2x LTV potential

**Option C: B2B Pivot**
- Target corporate eye care vouchers
- Undercut Specsavers corporate pricing 30-40%
- **Risk:** High (new channel) | **Return:** Unbounded

## 📚 Sources & Confidence
| Claim | Confidence | Source |
|-------|------------|--------|
| Trustpilot 4.8★ | ✅ High | Trustpilot.com direct |
| 70% cart abandonment | ✅ High | Baymard Institute industry data |
| 15-25% recovery rate | ⚠️ Medium | Industry benchmarks, varies by execution |
| Specsavers 42% share | ✅ High | Wikipedia citing 2012 data, still dominant |

Your call: Start with quick wins, or go straight to subscription?
```

## Quality Checklist

Before delivering any ROF report, verify:

- [ ] Started with TL;DR + clear recommendation(s)
- [ ] Presented multiple options (not single answer)
- [ ] Used tables for comparisons
- [ ] Every major claim has confidence label
- [ ] Included Devil's Advocate section
- [ ] Recommendations are actionable (not just informational)
- [ ] Sources cited for key claims
- [ ] Asked user for decision/next steps
